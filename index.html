About John Kennedy Otieno

John Kennedy Otieno is a Nairobi-based multimedia journalist, broadcast TV producer, and videographer with a track record spanning digital news, television, radio, and independent film. He has reported and published across beats including Kenya's governance landscape, economic policy, environmental accountability, legal developments, and technology — establishing himself as one of the sharper young voices in Kenyan journalism. His byline appears across 30 published pieces at Capital FM Kenya, and his broadcast work has aired on TV47.

As a broadcast journalist and TV producer, John has produced and presented investigative features for TV47 Kenya, tackling subjects from Kenya's informal hustle economy to labour rights and civic participation. His work as a videographer in Nairobi includes institutional production for USIU-Africa and independently directed short films — most notably Jane, a self-produced short that demonstrates full command of the filmmaking process from script to final cut.

John is also the creator and host of an audiovisual podcast exploring media, careers and ideas in the African context — with episodes featuring media industry figures including Abass Mpindi and Rachel Akidi. His institutional leadership credentials include reviving USIU Radio after a two-year closure, founding the inaugural USIU-Africa Media Week, and serving as Editor in Chief of the university yearbook — delivering a 196-page publication on time and to 1,200 print copies.

A multilingual communicator fluent in English, Kiswahili, French (DELF/DALF B2), Spanish and Luo, John brings rare range to cross-cultural storytelling across East Africa and beyond. His technical toolkit spans Adobe Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, DSLR camera operation, OSINT, digital verification, social media strategy, and CMS management.

John Kennedy Otieno is open to full-time journalism roles, freelance reporting commissions, video and documentary production, content strategy, and media training engagements across Kenya, East Africa and internationally. To commission work or discuss opportunities, reach him at jkennedyotieno@gmail.com or connect on LinkedIn.
